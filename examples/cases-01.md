# Case 01 — Strategic Realignment Under Hidden Conflict

---

## Scenario

A mid-sized company experienced stagnation despite consistent investment in marketing and operations.

Leadership believed the issue was execution inefficiency.

---

## Reality

The problem was not operational.

It was structural.

Hidden internal conflicts between decision-makers created:

- fragmented priorities  
- silent resistance  
- misaligned incentives  

---

## ARAM Analysis

### Power
Decision authority was unclear and informally distributed.

### Interest
Different stakeholders were optimizing for conflicting outcomes.

### Fear
Leadership avoided confronting internal misalignment.

### Illusion
The company believed growth depended on better execution.

### Hidden Opportunity
Realignment of decision structure could unlock growth without additional investment.

---

## Strategy

Instead of optimizing operations:

- redefine decision authority  
- align incentives  
- expose hidden conflicts  

---

## Outcome (Simulated)

- increased execution speed  
- reduced internal friction  
- improved strategic clarity  

---

## Insight

> The company did not need more effort.  
> It needed structural alignment.
