# Case 02 — Strategic Deconstruction of a High-Stakes Joint Venture

---

## Context

A joint venture was established in Campinas (Brazil) between:

- a Brazilian company with strong presence in Brazil and Europe  
- an Argentinian company with significant LATAM market share  

Together, they developed a software product in partnership with Autodesk, achieving:

- global innovation recognition  
- two international awards (FIMMA)  

Despite strong external validation, the internal system was deteriorating.

---

## Situation

The operation faced increasing strategic paralysis:

- decision-making bottlenecks  
- misaligned leadership structures  
- cross-cultural friction (Brazil–Argentina)  
- invisible conflicts affecting execution  

The organization believed the issue was operational.

It was not.

---

## Role

An external-embedded strategic intervention was conducted by Túlio Jannuzzelli under a non-hierarchical, immersive positioning:

- direct advisory to the CEO  
- strategic re-planning and execution  
- development of new initiatives and projects  
- high-level partnership structuring  

This positioning allowed unrestricted access to:

- decision flows  
- internal contradictions  
- informal power dynamics  

---

## ARAM Application

The intervention followed a non-linear cognitive strategy:

### 1. Deep Diagnostic (Beyond Surface Metrics)

Instead of analyzing performance indicators, the focus shifted to:

- hidden decision structures  
- informal influence networks  
- contradiction mapping  

---

### 2. Friction Mapping

Core tensions were identified:

- strategic vs operational priorities  
- national-cultural decision patterns  
- asymmetry in risk perception  

---

### 3. Contradiction Testing

Hypotheses were continuously stress-tested through:

- real-time strategic probes  
- behavioral observation  
- decision-response analysis  

Goal:
> expose structural truth through contradiction

---

### 4. Systemic Reframing

The organization was repositioned from:

> “underperforming operation”

to:

> “structurally misaligned system”

---

### 5. Executive Influence Layer

Critical insight:

The system could not be corrected incrementally.

It required **irreversible decisions**.

Strategic influence was applied directly at the CEO level to:

- confront avoided realities  
- clarify hidden risks  
- force decision clarity  

---

## Strategic Inflection Point

A key conclusion emerged:

> The joint venture structure itself had become the primary constraint.

Not the market.  
Not the product.  
Not execution.

The structure.

---

## Decision

Based on systemic analysis and sustained pressure-testing:

- the joint venture was dismantled  
- the Brazilian operation was repositioned  

This enabled its incorporation into Promob.

---

## Outcome (Strategic Level)

- removal of structural friction  
- restoration of decision clarity  
- strategic continuity under a new configuration  

---

## Meta Insight

> High-performance assets can coexist with structurally failing systems.

---

## ARAM Principle Demonstrated

> The deeper the system,  
> the less visible the real problem.

Storytelling:

# Case 02 — When the System Itself Becomes the Problem

---

## There was no visible failure.

The company was recognized.  
The product was awarded.  
The partnership was strong — at least on the surface.

A joint venture between a Brazilian and an Argentinian company, operating in Campinas, had achieved what most would consider success:

- international recognition  
- global innovation awards (FIMMA)  
- strategic partnership with Autodesk  

From the outside, everything worked.

Inside, nothing moved.

---

## The paralysis was subtle.

No dramatic collapse.  
No obvious breakdown.

Just a growing inability to:

- make decisions  
- execute with clarity  
- align leadership  

Meetings happened.  
Plans were made.  
But progress… stalled.

---

## The assumption was predictable:

“we need better execution”

That assumption was wrong.

---

## The entry point was not formal.

There was no predefined role.  
No standard consulting structure.

Instead, the intervention happened through an **immersive, extra-hierarchical position**:

- direct access to the CEO  
- unrestricted movement across decision layers  
- exposure to formal and informal dynamics  

That changed everything.

---

## What appeared first was not the problem —  
but the distortion around it.

Different leaders were solving different problems.

Silently.

Simultaneously.

In contradiction.

---

## The system was not inefficient.

It was misaligned.

---

## Through continuous observation, strategic probing, and contradiction testing, patterns began to emerge:

- authority existed, but was not clearly defined  
- decisions were made, but not owned  
- priorities were stated, but not shared  

And beneath all that:

> unspoken tension between structures, cultures, and control

---

## The joint venture created strength externally —  
but fragmentation internally.

Brazil and Argentina were not just markets.

They were **decision cultures**.

---

## At this point, improvement was no longer the question.

The real question became:

> “Is this system structurally viable?”

---

## That question changes everything.

Because it removes comfort.

It removes incremental thinking.

It forces confrontation.

---

## The process then shifted.

From analysis…  
to pressure.

---

## Hypotheses were tested in real conditions:

- decisions were challenged  
- reactions were observed  
- contradictions were amplified  

Not to create conflict.

But to reveal truth.

---

## And the truth was clear:

The joint venture itself had become the constraint.

Not the people.  
Not the product.  
Not the market.

The structure.

---

## At that level, there are no soft solutions.

Only decisive ones.

---

## The CEO was not lacking intelligence.

He was operating inside a distorted system.

Once the distortion became visible,  
the decision became inevitable.

---

## The outcome was not an optimization.

It was a structural break:

- the joint venture was dismantled  
- the Brazilian operation was repositioned  
- integration into Promob followed  

---

## From the outside, it looked like a business decision.

From the inside, it was a **system correction**.

---

## What changed was not the company.

What changed was the **clarity of reality**.

---

## Final Insight

Most organizations don’t fail because they lack capability.

They fail because they cannot see the system they are operating in.
